Momentum

Momentum is a physics term that relates to an object's amount of motion. If a thing is in motion (moving), it has momentum. It is defined as "mass in motion." Because all objects have mass, if an object moves, it has momentum - it has its mass in motion. The amount of momentum an object has is determined by two factors: how much stuff is moving and how fast it is moving. Momentum is determined by the variables mass and velocity.

Impluse

An impulse is a specific amount of force applied for a specific amount of time to induce a change in momentum.

Valence Electrons

Valence electrons are electrons found in an atom's outermost shell.
